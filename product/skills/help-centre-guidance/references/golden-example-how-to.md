## Before you start

Interview Evaluation generates three things from your interview transcript: a summary, a skills evaluation with scores, and candidate feedback. Each builds on the previous one.

If you link the interview to a role, the evaluation uses the Role Skills Profile as its framework - making scores and evidence more relevant. Without a role link, the AI infers which skills were being assessed.

> ℹ️ Not sure how meetings work in Spotted Zebra? See [Meetings](/link-to-meetings-article) for guidance on recording, uploading, and transcribing.

> ℹ️ New to Role Skills Profiles? See [What is a Role Skills Profile?](/link-to-rsp-article) to understand how they connect to evaluations.

## 1. Open your interview

Navigate to **Meetings** and select the interview you want to evaluate. The interview must show a status of "Ready" before you can access it.

<!-- ANNOTATION: Visual placement rule - screenshot appears immediately AFTER the instruction it illustrates. Never before. Descriptive alt text tells readers what they're looking at even if the image doesn't load. -->

> 📸 **Screenshot:** The Meetings list showing interview status indicators, with "Ready" highlighted.

## 2. Review the interview summary

The summary tab shows an AI-generated overview of the interview, formatted using your default template. The summary is editable - changes save automatically every 5 seconds.

> 📸 **Screenshot:** The summary tab showing an editable AI-generated summary.

Four default templates are available: Interview, Intake, Q&A, Screening, and General. Select **Change template** to switch. When you change the template, it becomes the default for all future meetings of that type.

<!-- ANNOTATION: Toggle sections manage page length. Use them for "nice to know" detail that would interrupt the flow of the main steps. The toggle title should be descriptive enough that users know whether they need to expand it. -->

### What each template does {toggle="true"}

| Template | Best for | What it covers |
|---|---|---|
| **Interview** | Hiring Managers, Interviewers | Experience, skills, motivation, and observations from a conversational interview |
| **Intake** | Recruiters | Key HR details, role requirements, ideal candidate profile, and hiring process |
| **Q&A** | All meeting types | Questions asked and answers given, ordered by question |
| **Screening** | Recruiters | Similar to Interview, with additional HR-specific information |
| **General** | Any meeting | High-level summary with key points and actions |

> ℹ️ Need a template that isn't listed? See [Creating custom Interview Summary Templates](/link-to-custom-templates) to set one up.

## 3. Link the interview to a role

<!-- ANNOTATION: This step is optional but important. We explain the "why" upfront so users understand the benefit before we ask them to do the work. -->

Linking your interview to a role and candidate connects the evaluation to the Role Skills Profile, making scores more specific and relevant. This step is optional but strongly recommended.

Open the **Skills evaluation** tab. You'll see up to three options:

- **Select a suggested role** - Spotted Zebra suggests up to three roles based on your meeting details. Selecting one loads the evaluation in a few seconds.
- **Manually select a role** - Browse your available roles by project. This takes 20-30 seconds to generate.
- **Generate without a role** - The AI infers which skills were assessed. This takes 30+ seconds.

> 📸 **Screenshot:** The Skills evaluation tab showing the three role-linking options.

<!-- ANNOTATION: ✅ callouts are for pro tips - things that improve the experience but aren't essential steps. -->

> ✅ **Tip:** Name your calendar events with "Interview" and the role name. This improves the accuracy of suggested role links.

### Manually selecting a role {toggle="true"}

Select **Link role** to browse roles by project. Only roles with interview stages appear in the list. Once you select a role, the candidate list for that role appears - select the candidate and save.

> 📸 **Screenshot:** The role and candidate selection drawer.

If the candidate isn't in the list, select **Add candidate** to add them directly from the interview. This adds them to Spotted Zebra without sending an assessment invite.

> 📸 **Screenshot:** The "Add candidate" option within the linking drawer.

## 4. Review the skills evaluation

<!-- ANNOTATION: This is the meatiest section. We break it into sub-sections with clear headings rather than one long wall of text. Each sub-section covers one concept. -->

The skills evaluation shows AI-generated scores and evidence for each skill identified in the interview.

### Skill scores

Each skill is scored on a 1-5 scale:

| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **1 - Not sufficiently capable** | Little or no positive evidence, or negative evidence significantly outweighs positive |
| **2 - Needs support** | Some positive evidence, but outweighed by stronger negative evidence |
| **3 - Capable** | Adequate competency demonstrated at the necessary level for the role |
| **4 - Strong** | Strong evidence beyond the minimum required for the role |
| **5 - Outstanding** | Exceptional evidence of expertise, with little to no negative evidence |
| **N/A - No evidence** | No evidence of the skill was demonstrated in the interview |

Select any score to change it manually. There is currently no way to reset to the original AI-assigned score.

> 📸 **Screenshot:** The skills evaluation showing scores for each skill, with one score being changed.

### How scores are calculated {toggle="true"}

Scores are based on three factors:

1. **Quality of evidence** - Stronger, more relevant examples carry more weight
2. **Quantity of evidence** - More supporting examples contribute, but with less impact than quality
3. **Role seniority** - Expectations adjust by level. A score of 5 for a junior role reflects different standards than a 5 for a senior role

The scoring system has been validated against real interview data across roles and seniority levels, and is continuously refined.

### Skill evidence

For each skill, evidence is categorised into areas of **strength** and **concern**. Select the link icon on any piece of evidence to jump to that moment in the transcript.

Evidence can be removed by selecting the **Remove** icon. Removing evidence from the evaluation does not remove it from the transcript.

> 📸 **Screenshot:** A skill detail panel showing strength and concern evidence, with the link and remove icons highlighted.

### How evidence is gathered {toggle="true"}

The AI considers four factors when extracting evidence:

- **Candidate's actions** - What specific actions did the candidate take in a given scenario?
- **Context of negative outcomes** - Were challenges beyond the candidate's control, or a result of their decisions?
- **Measurable outcomes** - Did the candidate provide clear, quantifiable results?
- **Individual contribution** - Is it clear the candidate personally demonstrated the skill, or was it a team effort?

### Evaluations linked to a Role Skills Profile

When linked to a role, the evaluation layout changes:

- Skills are categorised into the **"Right for" framework**
- Skills without AI evidence are grouped in a "without evidence" area - you can still score these manually and add notes
- You can move skills in and out of the **"Other skills"** area (hidden when shared) by hovering to show the Remove/Add buttons

> 📸 **Screenshot:** A linked evaluation showing the "Right for" categories and the "Other skills" area.

## 5. Make a recommended decision

Select your recommendation on the candidate's progression using the four-point scale:

- 👍👍 **Strong yes** - Advocate for hiring/progressing
- 👍 **Yes** - Should hire/progress
- 👎 **No** - Should not hire/progress
- 👎👎 **Strong no** - Advocate against hiring/progressing

<!-- ANNOTATION: "What happens next" is implicit here - the decision feeds into feedback generation in the next step. We make that connection explicit. -->

Your recommended decision is required before interview feedback can be generated in the next step.

---

## 6. Generate and review interview feedback

Interview feedback is AI-generated using the skills evaluation scores, evidence, and your recommended decision. It provides an editable, shareable summary you can send to colleagues and recruiters.

> 📸 **Screenshot:** Generated interview feedback based on skills evaluation and recommended decision.

If you update the recommended decision or skills evaluation after feedback has been generated, select **Regenerate** to update it.

<!-- ANNOTATION: ⚠️ callout for warnings. Direct but not alarming. -->

> ⚠️ Up to ten attempts to generate feedback can be made per interview.

### How feedback is structured {toggle="true"}

The recommended decision shapes the feedback structure:

- **Strong Yes** - Only positive feedback, reinforcing strengths
- **Yes** - Primarily positive, with significant concerns noted in a neutral tone
- **No / Strong No** - Opens with strengths, then outlines up to two areas where the candidate did not meet role expectations

If you manually adjust a skill score, the feedback adjusts accordingly - raising a score emphasises positive evidence; lowering it emphasises areas for improvement.

---

## 7. Share the evaluation

Select **Share** to send a read-only version of the interview to colleagues with Spotted Zebra accounts. You can configure which sections to include before generating the link.

> 📸 **Screenshot:** The sharing configuration panel showing toggleable sections.

The link is valid for 7 days and always shows the latest version of the evaluation. Any changes you make after sharing (scores, feedback, decisions) are reflected when the recipient views the link.

> ⚠️ The link is not accessible to candidates. There is currently no option to revoke a shared link before it expires.

## FAQs {toggle="true"}

| Question | Answer |
|---|---|
| Can my project team see my evaluation? | Only if you share it with them. See step 7 above. |
| Can I use skills evaluation on older interviews? | Skills evaluation, recommended decisions, and interview feedback were released in February 2025. Interviews recorded before this date do not have these features. |
| What if I want to change the linked role? | You can re-link an interview to a different role unless an evaluation has already been submitted (Standard and Limited access users). Full access users can re-link at any time. |
