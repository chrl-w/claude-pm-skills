## What are percentile scores?

Percentile scores rank a candidate's performance against a large global benchmark group (also called a "norm group"). A percentile score shows the percentage of people in the benchmark group that the candidate has outperformed.

For example, a candidate scoring at the 65th percentile has outperformed 65% of the benchmark group, with 35% scoring higher.

> 💡 **Percentiles aren't the same as percentages.** A percentage shows a fraction out of 100 (e.g. "answered 80% of questions correctly"). A percentile compares performance against other people - it's a ranking, not a raw score.

## Why it matters

Assessment scores give you an objective, data-informed starting point for evaluating candidates. They help you:

- Identify which skills a candidate is strongest and weakest in relative to the broader population
- Focus interview time on validating or probing specific areas
- Compare candidates consistently across the same role

Scores are one piece of the hiring puzzle - they're designed to be used alongside interviews, references, and other data sources, not as a standalone pass/fail gate.

## How scoring works

All Spotted Zebra assessment results form a normal distribution based on a benchmark of 650,000+ applicants. The average score is 50.

### High scores (75th percentile and above)

A score of 75+ is equivalent to an A grade. These candidates demonstrate strong capability in the assessed skill relative to the global benchmark. Scores at this level are uncommon across all skills simultaneously - a candidate who excels in every area is rare.

### Mid-range scores (30th to 70th percentile)

Candidates in this range show solid, reliable competency. This isn't a mediocre result - it signals someone with breadth across skills who could perform very well in the right role or with targeted development. Treat mid-range scores as an indicator of potential, not a deficit.

### Lower scores (below 30th percentile)

Scores below the 30th percentile highlight areas where a candidate may benefit from focused development or further assessment. They shouldn't be treated as a hard cutoff - they're a prompt to explore further in the interview.

## Key things to know

> ✅ **Use scores as conversation starters.** The most valuable use of percentile scores is in the interview. Probe a candidate's weaker areas to understand whether the score reflects a genuine gap or a limitation of the assessment context.

> ✅ **Don't expect perfection across all skills.** Candidates rarely score highly across every skill in all three "Right For" components. Focus on the skills that matter most for the specific role.

> ✅ **Scores adjust by norm group.** The benchmark group used can affect the distribution. Different norm groups may be applied depending on the role and assessment configuration.

> ℹ️ For more detail on how norm groups work and which one applies to your assessments, see [Norm Groups](/link-to-norm-groups).

## How it connects to your workflow

Assessment results feed into several other areas of the Spotted Zebra platform:

- **Interview Guides** use assessment scores to generate personalised, assessment-driven questions that probe a candidate's strengths and weaknesses
- **Interview Evaluation** combines assessment data with interview evidence for a holistic candidate view
